Charles Leclerc

Monegasque Formula One Driver for Scuderia Ferrari

Background Information Formula One Career Sources

Background Information

Born in Monaco, Leclerc grew up the middle of three sons and is the godson of the late F1 driver Jules Bianchi. His father, Herve, raced in Formula 3, and his younger brother, Arthur, is set to race in Formula 2 next season with DAMS Racing. He began carting in 2005 at the age of 8 in France, winning multiple karting championships and eventually finished second in the World KZ Championship behind current F1 world champion Max Verstappen.

He then graduated to junior formulae and in his first season, finished second in the Formula Renault Series. Leclerc went on to win the GP3 title in his first year, prompting his move to Formula 2 driving for Prema. He had an impressive season, claiming back-to-back pole positions and became the youngest ever champion in his rookie season.

Formula One Career

Sauber (2018)

Before making his formal appearance in F1, Leclerc was in the Ferrari Driver Academy and was a test driver for Haas and Scuderia Ferrari. In his first season, he drove for Sauber F1, giving a dominant performance against his much more experienced teammate Marcus Ericsson. Aside from some rocky drives mid-season, Leclerc was able to score points consistently in a car that wasn't capable of doing so, with his best finish being P6 in Azerbaijan and 3 consecutive P7 performances at the end of the season. Ferrari soon signed him for the 2019 season, replacing Kimi Raikonnen.

Ferrari (2019 - present)

In his first season for the Scuderia, Leclerc showed great promise, scoring the most pole positions of any drivers and finishing fourth in the championship ahead of his teammate Vettel. Highlights this season include his maiden win in Spa after the tragic death of his friend, F2 driver Anthoine Hubert, and his win in Monza in front of the Ferrari home crowd.

Leclerc's second win in his career, Monza 2019

Leclerc's next season was much less fortuitous as Ferrari produced an underwhelming midfield car. Due to several mistakes, including a first lap collision with Vettel leading to a Ferrari double retirement in Austria, he ended the season in 8th. The next year he was partnered with Carlos Sainz and had a better season, though he could not match his rookie performance. Leclerc only podiumed once and had a disappointing home GP where he took pole but suffered an engine failure before the start of the race, and he ultimately finished 7th in the championship behind his teammate. In the 2022 season, Leclerc returned as a title contender, winning 2 out of the first 3 races, including a Ferrari 1-2 in the opening race. However, the latter half of the mid-season saw the title slipping away from him due to strategic errors by Ferrari. He ended the season in 2nd, after a dramatic fight for the position in the final race against Perez, and brought his team to 2nd in the Constructors' Championship.

Leclerc finished second in the 2022 World Drivers' Championship
